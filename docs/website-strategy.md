# Website Strategy

## Purpose

The Lumisync website is a product website, not a decorative landing page.

It should explain the problem, show the product, build trust, and guide different audiences toward the right next step.

## Primary Audiences

Students need to understand how Lumisync makes daily campus life easier.

University leaders need to trust that Lumisync is serious, accessible, secure, and scalable.

Investors need to see a differentiated product vision with a credible expansion path.

Recruiters and engineers need to see thoughtful execution, not a generic AI wrapper.

## Positioning

Lumisync should be positioned as an AI-powered Campus Operating System.

Avoid positioning it as only:

- a campus map
- an AI chatbot
- a student app
- a Texas Tech tool
- a university directory

Those are modules inside the larger product.

## Page Responsibilities

The homepage should communicate the full product promise quickly and show real product surfaces.

The map page should make the Campus Engine feel like a flagship feature, with grounded claims about buildings, search, directions, accessibility, and future indoor navigation.

The Lumi page should explain AI assistance as trusted campus guidance, not generic chatbot magic.

The universities or enterprise pages should speak to scalability, administration, security, accessibility, and data quality.

The student-facing pages should emphasize practical daily tasks: finding places, checking dining, discovering events, and asking campus questions.

The roadmap page should separate shipped, in-progress, and future capabilities.

## Conversion Goals

The website should support these actions:

- join the beta
- contact the team
- understand the product
- explore universities
- evaluate enterprise potential
- read product documentation

Calls to action should be specific and contextual. Avoid repeating the same generic CTA on every section.

## Product Truth Standard

Marketing claims must match product reality.

If a feature is not implemented yet, describe it as planned, future, upcoming, or on the roadmap.

If a feature depends on university data availability, say that clearly in the relevant context.

Trust is more valuable than hype.
